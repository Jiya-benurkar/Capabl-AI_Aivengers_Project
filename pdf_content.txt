Team Size: 3–4 students | Duration: 8 Weeks | Choose Your Challenge Level 
Project Overview 
Build an AI-powered academic assistant that serves as both a comprehensive 
subject guide and question bank explainer. Students can upload lecture notes, 
textbooks, lab manuals, and previous year question papers to create a complete 
learning companion. Ask "Explain Database Normalization with examples" or "How 
to solve Q3 from 2023 DBMS paper" and get comprehensive answers combining 
theory, examples, and practice problems from all uploaded materials. 
Domain Focus: Educational Technology & Comprehensive Academic Learning 
 Core Skills: Multi-source RAG, academic content synthesis, topic understanding, 
exam preparation, intelligent content organization

Frequently Asked Questions 
Q: 
How 
is 
this 
different 
from 
just 
a 
question 
bank 
explainer? 
 A: This provides complete topic understanding combining textbooks, notes, labs, 
AND question papers. Students get comprehensive learning, not just exam 
preparation. It's like having a personal tutor for each subject. 
Q: How do I handle different types of academic content effectively? 
 A: Implement content type detection (theory/practical/examples), create source-
aware explanations, maintain content hierarchy, and provide multi-perspective 
topic coverage from all available sources. 
Q: What about ensuring comprehensive and accurate topic explanations? 
 A: Cross-reference multiple sources, implement confidence scoring, show all 
contributing materials, maintain source attribution, and provide comprehensive 
coverage indicators for topic completeness. 
Q: 
Can 
we 
switch 
tracks 
mid-project? 
 A: Yes, from B to A after Week 4 checkpoint. Consider the complexity of advanced 
learning analytics and multi-document synthesis when deciding. 
Q: 
How 
do 
I 
organize 
content 
from 
multiple 
sources 
coherently? 
 A: Create topic taxonomies, implement content deduplication, maintain source 
attribution, build knowledge graphs, and provide structured learning progressions 
from basic to advanced concepts. 
Q: What about handling different academic subjects and their unique 
requirements? 
 A: Track A: Focus on general academic content patterns. Track B: Implement 
 
 
subject-specific processing (CS code examples, Math equations, Science 
diagrams) and adaptive explanation formats. 
Q: How do I measure learning effectiveness and content comprehensiveness? 
 A: Track topic coverage completeness, implement comprehension assessment, 
measure query resolution success, analyze content gap identification, and provide 
learning progress analytics. 
Q: What about integration with existing learning management systems? 
 A: Track A: Focus on standard content export formats. Track B: Implement basic 
LMS integration capabilities and standard educational data exchange formats. 
Q: How do I handle copyright and fair use with multiple academic sources? 
 A: Focus on user-uploaded content, provide proper attribution systems, implement 
fair use guidelines, never redistribute copyrighted materials, and encourage 
institutional content policies. 
Q: 
What 
about 
offline 
functionality 
for 
comprehensive 
learning? 
 A: Implement content caching for core topics, provide offline content viewing, 
maintain basic query capabilities, and ensure synchronization when connectivity 
returns for seamless learning experiences.
